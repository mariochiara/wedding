Location

The Abbey

A Cistercian monastery in the province of Latina, central Italy, near the towns of Sermoneta and Ninfa. The abbey is located at 116 m a.s.l., it is one of the greatest masterpieces of its kind in the province after the abbey of Fossanova. According to tradition, the abbey was founded in the twelfth century by Greek monks; in the thirteenth century it was occupied and restored by the Templars. When in the 14th century this order was dissolved, the Cistercians took over.

Latina

The capital of the province of Latina in the Lazio region, in central Italy. It's the second-largest city of the region, after the national capital Rome.

La navata centrale

L'interno della chiesa, a tre navate suddivise da pilastri e colonne, presenta pareti spoglie di affreschi secondo i canoni del "memento mori" dei cistercensi che evitavano gli sfarzi architettonici perché non contava per loro la materialità ma, invece, la spiritualità.
